2. Card 1: The Mechanical Injury (របួសផ្នែកមេកានិច):

What it is: Explain clearly and scientifically that an obstetric fistula is a hole between the birth canal and the bladder or rectum.

The Cause: It is caused by prolonged, obstructed labor (sometimes lasting days) without medical intervention. The intense physical pressure of the baby’s head against the mother's pelvis cuts off blood flow, causing the soft tissue to die and create a hole, leaving the woman incontinent.

3. Card 2: A Measure of Absolute Poverty (រង្វាស់នៃភាពក្រីក្រតោកយ៉ាក):

The Preventable Tragedy: Emphasize that obstetric fistula is 100% preventable and 100% curable. It has been entirely eradicated in wealthy countries for over a century because of the Cesarean section (C-section).

Structural Violence: Explain that this injury is the ultimate sign of absolute poverty. It only happens to women who live too far from a hospital, lack money for transport, or live in areas with broken healthcare systems.

4. Card 3: Maternal Mortality in Cambodia & The World (អត្រាមរណៈមាតានៅកម្ពុជា និងពិភពលោក):

The Near-Miss: Explain that fistula is often called a 'near-miss' for maternal mortality. For every woman who survives with a fistula, many others die in childbirth.

The Progress: Note that Cambodia has made incredible strides in reducing maternal mortality over the last two decades by building rural health centers and training midwives, but remote populations still face severe risks.
