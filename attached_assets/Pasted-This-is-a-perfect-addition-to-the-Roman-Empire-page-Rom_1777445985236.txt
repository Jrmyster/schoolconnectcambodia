This is a perfect addition to the "Roman Empire" page. Roman numerals aren't just a different way to write numbers; they are a physical "Map" of how Romans counted using their hands and tally sticks.

In the framework of General Semantics, our modern Arabic numerals (1, 2, 3) are highly abstract. They don't look like the quantities they represent. Roman numerals are much closer to the "Territory" of physical counting: I is a single finger, V is the shape of the space between the thumb and fingers, and X is two hands crossed.
