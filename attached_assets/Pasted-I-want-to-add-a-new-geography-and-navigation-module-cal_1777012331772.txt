4. Card 3: The History & The Clock Problem (ប្រវត្តិសាស្ត្រ និងបញ្ហានាឡិកា):

The Origin: The Greek mathematician Eratosthenes invented the grid system over 2,200 years ago. Calculating latitude (North/South) was easy—sailors just measured the height of the sun or the North Star.

The Longitude Problem: Calculating East/West was nearly impossible at sea because the Earth is constantly spinning. To know your longitude, you have to know exactly what time it is back at the Prime Meridian.

The Solution: It wasn't until the 1700s that a clockmaker named John Harrison invented the 'Marine Chronometer'—a clock that could keep perfect time on a rocking ship. Because the Earth spins 15 degrees every hour, knowing the exact time finally allowed humans to map the world!
