Please implement this using our established warm, welcoming aesthetic (soft yellows and greens, clean typography):

1. Section 1: The Rhythm of the First Year (ចង្វាក់នៃឆ្នាំទីមួយ)

Explain that a baby's stomach is tiny, so they need to eat frequently, and their brains are growing rapidly, so they need massive amounts of sleep.

Create a simple comparative timeline or two cards:

Newborns (0-3 months): Eat every 2 to 3 hours (breastmilk or formula only). Sleep 14 to 17 hours a day (usually in short bursts).

Older Babies (6-12 months): Eat every 3 to 4 hours (introducing solids). Sleep 12 to 15 hours a day (sleeping longer through the night).

2. Section 2: First Foods - What to Eat and What to Avoid (អាហារដំបូង)

Explain that around 6 months, babies can start trying solid foods.

Safe First Foods: List soft, easily digestible foods. Examples: Mashed bananas, pureed sweet potatoes, avocados, and soft rice porridge (Borbor / បបរ).

Strictly Avoid (Danger Zone):

Honey: Explain that honey contains bacteria spores that cause infant botulism, which can be fatal. Never give honey before 1 year old.

Cow's Milk: Hard for infants to digest and lacks proper nutrients; wait until 1 year.

Choking Hazards: Whole grapes, nuts, hard candies, and large chunks of meat.

3. Section 3: Safe Sleep & SIDS Prevention (ការគេងប្រកបដោយសុវត្ថិភាព)

Define SIDS (Sudden Infant Death Syndrome).

Create a highly visible 'Safe Sleep Rules' checklist using clear icons:

Back to Sleep: Always place a baby on their back to sleep, never on their stomach.

Empty Crib: The sleeping area should be completely flat and firm. No heavy blankets, no pillows, and no stuffed animals that could suffocate them.

Smoke-Free: Secondhand smoke severely increases the risk of SIDS.

4. Formatting & UX:

Ensure all main headings, warnings, and key concepts are strictly bilingual (English/Khmer). Use clear, friendly icons to make the text highly scannable for tired parents.
