2. Section 1: Key Facts (Use a Card Grid Layout):

No Active Pump: Unlike the blood circulatory system, it has no heart to pump it. It relies on muscle contraction, movement, and deep breathing to circulate lymph fluid.

Fluid Drainage & Volume: It collects extra fluid (lymph) that leaks from blood vessels into tissues, returning it to the blood to prevent swelling. It manages nearly double the volume of fluid compared to the blood circulation.

Filters Pathogens: The body contains roughly 400-800 lymph nodes that filter lymph, trapping bacteria, viruses, and abnormal cells (like cancer cells).

Immune Defense: It produces and transports lymphocytes (T-cells and B-cells) to fight infection.

Lymph Nodes Swell: When you have an infection, lymph nodes swell because they produce more white blood cells and accumulate pathogens.

Fat Absorption: It helps absorb fats and nutrients from the digestive system and transports them to the bloodstream.

Common Conditions: If the system fails to drain fluid correctly, it can cause swelling known as lymphedema. Other diseases include lymphoma, which is a cancer of the lymphatic system.
