2. The Bilingual Data Bank (ទិន្នន័យទ្វេភាសា):
Populate the cards with the following sociologists, translating the core concepts into simple, accessible language for students:

Karl Marx (កាល់ ម៉ាក្ស):

The Big Idea: Class Conflict (ជម្លោះវណ្ណៈ)

Concept: Argued that society is driven by the struggle between the wealthy owners who control resources and the workers who provide the labor.

Émile Durkheim (អេមីល ឌៀកខែម):

The Big Idea: Social Solidarity (សាមគ្គីភាពសង្គម)

Concept: Studied what keeps society from falling apart. He believed shared values, traditions, and institutions act as the 'glue' holding us together.

Max Weber (ម៉ាក់ស៍ វេប៊ឺ):

The Big Idea: Rationalization & Status (ហេតុផលនីយកម្ម និងឋានៈ)

Concept: Looked beyond just money, exploring how religious ideas, culture, and social status shape human behavior and the economy.

Robert K. Merton (រ៉ូបឺត ខេ មឺតុន):

The Big Idea: Unintended Consequences (ផលវិបាកដែលមិនបានព្រាងទុក)

Concept: Pointed out that social actions often have hidden or accidental results (Latent Functions) that are different from their obvious goals.

C. Wright Mills (ស៊ី រ៉ាយ មីលស៍):

The Big Idea: The Sociological Imagination (ការស្រមើស្រមៃផ្នែកសង្គមវិទ្យា)

Concept: Taught us to see the connection between our private, personal struggles and the massive historical forces of the world.

Pierre Bourdieu (ព្យែរ បូរឌីយូ):

The Big Idea: Cultural Capital (ទុនវប្បធម៌)

Concept: Showed that knowing how to talk, dress, and appreciate art gives people an invisible advantage in life, just like having money.

Anthony Giddens (អាន់តូនី ហ្គីដដិនស៍):

The Big Idea: Structuration (រចនាសម្ព័ន្ធនីយកម្ម)

Concept: Argued that we are shaped by society's rules, but through our daily actions, we also have the power to change those rules.
